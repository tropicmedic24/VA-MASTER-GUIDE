Getting Started: Foundation of Your Claim

Pre-Filing Medical Treatment - Critical First Step

This Cannot Be Overstated: Getting medical treatment BEFORE filing your claim is absolutely essential, regardless of whether you see a VA doctor or civilian provider.

Why Pre-Filing Treatment Matters:

  • Establishes Current Diagnosis: Shows your condition exists today
  • Documents Severity: Medical records show how the condition affects you
  • Creates Paper Trail: Provides evidence of ongoing symptoms
  • Strengthens Your Case: Recent treatment records support your claim

Pre-Filing Checklist:

Schedule medical appointments for all conditions you plan to claim
Obtain copies of all military medical records
Gather civilian medical records
Document symptoms and how they affect daily life
Contact potential buddy statement witnesses

Medical Evidence: Building Your Case

Types of Medical Evidence

  • Service Medical Records: All treatment received while on active duty
  • VA Medical Records: All VA hospital and clinic visits
  • Private Medical Records: Civilian doctor visits and treatments
  • Diagnostic Tests: X-rays, MRIs, blood tests, etc.

Key Medical Evidence Requirements:

  • Current Diagnosis: Medical professional must diagnose your condition
  • In-Service Event: Evidence something happened during military service
  • Nexus: Medical opinion connecting your current condition to military service

Nexus Letters: The Critical Connection

A nexus letter is a medical opinion from a qualified healthcare provider that connects your current medical condition to your military service. It's often the most important piece of evidence in your claim.

Key Elements of a Strong Nexus Letter:

  • Written by a qualified medical professional
  • Reviews your military and medical history
  • Provides medical opinion on causation
  • Uses language like "more likely than not" or "at least as likely as not"
  • Explains the medical reasoning

Buddy Statements: Personal Testimony

Buddy statements are written testimony from people who witnessed your condition or its effects. They can be from fellow service members, family, friends, or coworkers.

Effective Buddy Statement Tips:

  • Include specific examples and dates
  • Describe observable symptoms and behaviors
  • Explain how symptoms have changed over time
  • Be honest and factual
  • Include witness contact information

VA Disability Rating Chart

The VA uses a unique system for combining ratings called "VA Math" - they don't simply add percentages.

How VA Math Works:

  • Start with your highest single rating
  • Calculate remaining efficiency (100% - current rating)
  • Apply next rating to remaining efficiency
  • Round to nearest 10% at the end

Personal Statement: Your Story Matters

A personal statement is your opportunity to tell the VA in your own words how your service-connected conditions affect your daily life. It's a powerful piece of evidence that provides context beyond medical records.

Effective Personal Statement Tips:

  • Be specific about symptoms and their frequency
  • Describe how conditions affect work, relationships, and daily activities
  • Include specific examples and incidents
  • Mention any functional limitations
  • Be honest about good days and bad days
  • Use clear, straightforward language

Important Reminders:

  • Focus on how conditions affect you TODAY
  • Don't exaggerate - be truthful and accurate
  • Include impact on employment and relationships
  • Mention any accommodations you need

6 Critical Points Every Veteran Must Know

üîë Key Point #1: VA Raters Are NOT Doctors

Reality Check: VA raters are data reviewers, not medical professionals. They read through your file and make decisions based on the evidence you provide. You must paint a complete picture through your evidence.

üìù Key Point #2: Write Your Own Story - Focus on Your WORST Days

Critical Strategy: Raters don't know you personally. When describing your conditions, always focus on your worst days, flare-ups, and most severe symptoms. This isn't lying - it's showing the full impact of your disability.

‚úçÔ∏è Key Point #3: Keep It Simple Yet Specific

The Balance: Use clear, everyday language while being extremely specific about your symptoms and limitations.

Good Example: "My back pain flares up 3-4 times per week. On these days, I cannot bend over to tie my shoes without severe stabbing pain that lasts 2-3 hours."

üìã Key Point #4: Recent Medical Records Are GOLD

Priority #1: Get recent medical treatment BEFORE filing your claim. Records from the last 6-12 months carry much more weight than old records.

üí∞ Key Point #5: The Funding Is Already There

Game Changer: Congress has already allocated funds for veteran disability benefits. The money exists - you just need to prove your disability qualifies you for it.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Point #6: Reference Title 38 USC Laws in Your Claims

Legal Foundation: Title 38 of the United States Code contains all the laws governing veteran benefits. Referencing specific USC laws in your personal statements and letters strengthens your legal position.

Key Title 38 Sections to Know:

  • 38 USC ¬ß 1110: Basic entitlement to compensation
  • 38 USC ¬ß 1131: Service-connection for disabilities
  • 38 USC ¬ß 1154(a): Benefit of the doubt standard
  • 38 USC ¬ß 5107(b): Reasonable doubt resolved in favor of veteran

Crucial Points

Advanced strategies that can make or break your claim

‚ö° Crucial Point #1: ALWAYS Appeal If Denied

The Strategy: Don't accept a denial as final. Follow this proven appeal sequence:

  • Step 1: File a Higher-Level Review first (different rater reviews same evidence)
  • Step 2: If still denied, file a Supplemental Claim with NEW evidence
  • Step 3: Board of Veterans Appeals if needed

Reality: Many veterans get approved on appeal with the same evidence. Different raters can reach different conclusions.

ü©∫ Crucial Point #2: Get Nexus Letters from Civilian Doctors

Why This Works: Civilian doctors aren't employed by the VA and have no financial incentive to deny your claim. They're often more willing to write strong nexus letters.

üíª Crucial Point #3: Telemedicine Is Your Secret Weapon

Game Changer: Use telehealth appointments to document symptoms regularly. Whether VA or private providers, these visits create ongoing medical records.

  • Easier to schedule than in-person visits
  • Creates consistent documentation
  • Shows ongoing treatment and symptoms
  • Can be done from home during flare-ups

üì¢ Crucial Point #4: Always Speak to Your Worst Day

Critical Communication Strategy: When talking to doctors, C&P examiners, or filling out forms, ALWAYS describe your symptoms based on your worst days.

I firmly believe that [his/her/their] plantar fasciitis was caused by [his/her/their] active duty service, and I fully support this claim.
