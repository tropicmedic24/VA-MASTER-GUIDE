🔹 THE VA CLAIM PROCESS (SIMPLIFIED)

Follow these 5 essential steps to maximize your chances of approval.

📝 Step 1: File Your Claim

  • Go to va.gov - Use the online portal for fastest processing
  • File a new claim or increase online
  • Attach: Service records, DD-214, medical records

📋 Step 2: Submit Your Statement in Support of Claim

This is CRUCIAL - Explain why your condition is connected to military service.

👥 Step 3: Attach Buddy Letters

Get statements from spouses, coworkers, fellow soldiers, NCOs.

🩺 Step 4: Submit or Request a Nexus Letter

Must use "at least as likely as not" language.

🏥 Step 5: Prepare for C&P Exam

Describe your worst days - not your best days.

Getting Started: Foundation of Your Claim

Pre-Filing Medical Treatment - Critical First Step

This Cannot Be Overstated: Getting medical treatment BEFORE filing your claim is absolutely essential.

Pre-Filing Checklist:

Schedule medical appointments for all conditions
Obtain military medical records
Gather civilian medical records
Document symptoms and daily impacts
Contact buddy statement witnesses

Medical Evidence: Building Your Case

Types of Medical Evidence

  • Service Medical Records: All treatment during active duty
  • VA Medical Records: All VA hospital visits
  • Private Medical Records: Civilian doctor visits
  • Diagnostic Tests: X-rays, MRIs, blood tests

Key Requirements:

  • Current Diagnosis: Medical professional must diagnose your condition
  • In-Service Event: Evidence something happened during service
  • Nexus: Medical opinion connecting condition to service

Nexus Letters: The Critical Connection

A nexus letter connects your current medical condition to your military service. Often the most important piece of evidence.

Key Elements of a Strong Nexus Letter:

  • Written by a qualified medical professional
  • Reviews your military and medical history
  • Provides medical opinion on causation
  • Uses language like "more likely than not" or "at least as likely as not"
  • Explains the medical reasoning

Buddy Statements: Personal Testimony

Written testimony from people who witnessed your condition or its effects.

Effective Buddy Statement Tips:

  • Include specific examples and dates
  • Describe observable symptoms and behaviors
  • Explain how symptoms have changed over time
  • Be honest and factual
  • Include witness contact information

VA Disability Rating Chart

The VA uses "VA Math" - they don't simply add percentages.

How VA Math Works:

  • Start with your highest single rating
  • Calculate remaining efficiency (100% - current rating)
  • Apply next rating to remaining efficiency
  • Round to nearest 10% at the end

Personal Statement: Your Story Matters

Tell the VA in your own words how your conditions affect your daily life.

Effective Personal Statement Tips:

  • Be specific about symptoms and their frequency
  • Describe how conditions affect work, relationships, and daily activities
  • Include specific examples and incidents
  • Mention any functional limitations
  • Be honest about good days and bad days
  • Use clear, straightforward language

6 Critical Points Every Veteran Must Know

🔑 Key Point #1: VA Raters Are NOT Doctors

VA raters are data reviewers. You must paint a complete picture through evidence.

📝 Key Point #2: Write Your Own Story - Focus on WORST Days

Always describe your worst days, flare-ups, and most severe symptoms.

✍️ Key Point #3: Keep It Simple Yet Specific

Use clear language while being extremely specific about limitations.

📋 Key Point #4: Recent Medical Records Are GOLD

Get recent treatment BEFORE filing. Records from last 6-12 months are crucial.

💰 Key Point #5: The Funding Is Already There

Congress allocated the funds. You just need to prove your disability qualifies.

⚖️ Key Point #6: Reference Title 38 USC Laws

Cite specific USC laws in your statements to strengthen legal position.

  • 38 USC § 1110: Basic entitlement to compensation
  • 38 USC § 1131: Service-connection for disabilities
  • 38 USC § 1154(a): Benefit of the doubt standard
  • 38 USC § 5107(b): Reasonable doubt resolved in favor of veteran
